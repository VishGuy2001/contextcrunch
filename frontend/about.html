About

Why I built this

While working on AI-heavy projects, I started noticing something that wasn’t obvious at first — I wasn’t using these tools efficiently.

Across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini, the same patterns kept showing up. Context would quietly fill up mid-session. Conversations would become repetitive. Tokens were being used on things I didn’t actually need.

Sometimes I’d lose important context completely. Other times I’d hit limits faster than expected — even on paid plans — and either have to wait hours or pay more just to continue.

At a small scale, it’s frustrating.
At a larger scale, it becomes expensive very quickly.

The problem

There’s no clear visibility into how these systems are actually being used.

  • How much context you’re consuming
  • Where tokens are being wasted
  • How efficiently your prompts are structured

Most people use AI tools daily, but have no real insight into what’s happening behind the scenes.

What ContextCrunch does

ContextCrunch was built to make that visible.

  • See how efficiently you’re using AI models
  • Identify where tokens are being wasted
  • Structure prompts to reduce redundancy
  • Keep context cleaner and more useful over time

Why this matters

For individuals, it means saving time, avoiding lost context, and learning how to use AI tools more effectively.

For teams and businesses, it means understanding where AI costs are going — and fixing inefficiencies before they scale.

The bigger idea

Most AI tools focus on output.

ContextCrunch focuses on how you get there — making AI usage more transparent and intentional.

In short

I built ContextCrunch because I ran into these inefficiencies myself — and there wasn’t a simple way to measure or fix them.

This is both a learning tool for anyone using AI, and a practical way to make that usage more efficient.
